## A Beginning

On November 6, 2017, I received a life-changing email.

I was offered a seat in the **Google Developer Scholarship Challenge** hosted through Udacity.

At the time, I had no background in computer science and no experience developing software professionally. What began as curiosity quickly became a passion for building technology and solving problems through code.

Looking back, this scholarship marked the beginning of a journey that would later lead to deep learning, reinforcement learning, data science, artificial intelligence, and ultimately an MSc in Artificial Intelligence.

I remain deeply grateful to both Google and Udacity for making that opportunity possible.

## Why This Repository Matters

Technically, these are some of the oldest projects in my portfolio.

Historically, they are among the most important.

They document the transition from complete beginner to independent developer and represent the foundation upon which later work in machine learning, AI systems, computational research, and technical education was built.

Many later milestones—including deep learning, reinforcement learning, and AI systems development—trace back to the opportunity created by the Google Developer Scholarship.

## Historical Context

These projects were originally developed between 2017 and 2018 as part of the Android Basics Nanodegree and subsequent self-directed learning.
